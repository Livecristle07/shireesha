BMS(Battery management system)

The Battery Management System (BMS) is a comprehensive solution that addresses various critical aspects of battery performance and safety. It includes state-of-the-art features such as state of charge (SOC) estimation, state of health (SOH) monitoring, cell balancing for optimal battery life, over/under voltage protection, and efficient thermal management.

The BMS also incorporates current limiting to prevent overload, advanced fault detection mechanisms for safety, and CAN communication for seamless integration into vehicle systems. Furthermore, IoT communication capabilities enable remote monitoring and control, making the BMS a cutting-edge solution for modern battery management needs.

EV Chargers

EV charges, or Electric Vehicle charges, are devices or stations designed to recharge the batteries of electric vehicles (EVs). These charging stations supply electricity to the EV, allowing it to store energy for continued operation. There are several types of EV charges, including Level 1, Level 2, and DC fast chargers, each with varying power levels and charging speeds.

Level 1 chargers typically use a standard household outlet and provide a slow charging rate suitable for overnight charging.

Level 2 chargers offer faster charging speeds, often found at public charging stations and in residential settings with upgraded power outlets.

Level 3 or DC fast chargers provide rapid charging, making them suitable for quick top-ups during long trips.
